🦠 What is NDM and Why Does It Matter?

NDM = New Delhi Metallo-beta-lactamase

NNew Delhi — first identified 2008 in a patient returning from India (Lancet Infect Dis)
DDestroys carbapenems — hydrolyses the beta-lactam ring of meropenem, imipenem, ertapenem
MMobile — plasmid-mediated; horizontally transferred between organisms in the ICU

NDM+ organisms are resistant to virtually all beta-lactams and typically fluoroquinolones and aminoglycosides too. Polymyxins are the only reliable backbone.

01 Diagnose VAP — Clinical Criteria + BAL
VAP definition: new pneumonia ≥48h after endotracheal intubation.

Clinical criteria: new CXR infiltrate + ≥2 of: fever >38°C, leukocytosis/leucopaenia, new purulent secretions, worsening oxygenation.

BAL is superior to tracheal aspirate for organism identification and quantitative culture. Diagnostic threshold: ≥10⁴ CFU/mL from BAL.

Send: Gram stain, quantitative culture, sensitivity including carbapenem susceptibility and molecular beta-lactamase typing.

02 Bedside FOB — Diagnosis AND Therapy
FOB served two purposes: BAL specimen collection AND therapeutic bronchial toileting of the mucus plug causing LLL collapse.

FOB in a ventilated patient:
• Switch to volume-controlled ventilation — bronchoscope occupies ETT lumen → increased resistance → TV drops in pressure modes
• Increase FiO₂ during procedure
• Anticipate rising peak pressures
• LLL re-expanded on CXR within 12 hours of plug removal

03 Antibiotic Strategy — Polymyxin Backbone
Colistin (Polymyxin E): administered as colistimethate sodium (CMS) — inactive prodrug hydrolysed in vivo. Disrupts Gram-negative outer membrane.

CMS 100 mg IV BD (weight-adjusted). Nephrotoxicity in 30–50% — daily creatinine monitoring mandatory.

Combination to reduce resistance emergence:
• Colistin + minocycline 200 mg BD + tigecycline (off-label)
• Inhaled colistin — higher local airway concentrations than systemic delivery alone

Monotherapy = resistance emergence. Microbiologist input is essential and non-negotiable.

04 Infection Control — Contact Precautions
NDM is plasmid-mediated — horizontally transferred between organisms via hands and contaminated equipment. A single index case can seed an entire unit.

Non-negotiable: contact precautions, dedicated nursing, apron + gloves for all contact, soap-and-water hand hygiene (Acinetobacter survives on dry surfaces for weeks — alcohol gel insufficient alone).

Notify infection control immediately.

05 Ventilator Weaning — ABCDE Bundle
A — Analgesia first: adequate pain control before reducing sedation
B — Breathing: daily SBT (PS 8 / PEEP 5, 30–120 min). Pass: RR <35, SpO₂ >90%, no distress, haemodynamically stable
C — Coordination: daily sedation holds + neurological assessment
D — Delirium screening (CAM-ICU)
E — Early mobility and exercise

Patient passed first SBT Day 14. Extubated to HFNO Day 15. Total ventilation: 17 days. Inflammatory markers normalising Day 10.

🎤 Viva Questions

What is NDM and why does it make Acinetobacter so difficult to treat? ▶
NDM is a plasmid-encoded carbapenemase that hydrolyses and inactivates all carbapenem antibiotics. NDM+ Acinetobacter is also typically resistant to fluoroquinolones and aminoglycosides — the therapeutic window narrows to polymyxins ± tigecycline/minocycline. Crucially, NDM is plasmid-mediated → horizontal transfer to other species in the ICU is possible, making containment critical.
Why is colistin nephrotoxic and how do you manage this risk? ▶
Colistin accumulates in proximal renal tubular cells and disrupts cellular membrane integrity (same mechanism as antibacterial action on bacterial outer membranes) → dose-dependent proximal tubular injury and AKI. Incidence 30–50%. Management: daily creatinine monitoring, avoid co-nephrotoxins, adequate hydration, renal dose-adjustment, and add inhaled colistin as adjunct to reduce systemic doses where possible.
What is the most important single intervention for ventilator weaning? ▶
The daily spontaneous breathing trial (SBT). Multiple RCTs (Esteban et al., NEJM 1995) demonstrated daily SBT with PS 8 / PEEP 5 significantly reduces ventilator duration compared to gradual weaning. Pass criteria: RR <35, SpO₂ >90%, no haemodynamic deterioration, no accessory muscle use. A patient who passes their first SBT should be extubated that day unless there is a specific contraindication.
Why adjust ventilator settings during bedside FOB in a ventilated patient? ▶
The bronchoscope occupies a significant portion of the ETT lumen, dramatically increasing airway resistance. In pressure-controlled modes, tidal volume drops as pressure cannot overcome the elevated resistance. Switching to volume-controlled ventilation ensures consistent TV delivery. FiO₂ is increased to compensate for the additional dead space and V/Q mismatch during suctioning. These adjustments are reversed once the scope is removed.
