𓁹 Anubis & The Anaesthetist 𓁹

Anubis — the jackal-headed psychopomp — guided souls across the threshold between the living and the dead, not choosing who died but ensuring safe passage through the unknown.

The anaesthetist walks a similar path — every patient who surrenders consciousness crosses a threshold, and we guide them in, and guide them out.

Trauma anaesthesia adds another layer: these patients arrive broken — in body and spirit. We hold the physiological chaos together long enough for healing to begin. Broken bones come with broken souls. We treat both.

"He who guides the living through the unknown bears the same duty as he who guides the dead — to carry the soul safely across."
— Adapted from Egyptian Funerary Texts

TraumaDog was built during one month of FNB Exit Examination preparation in Trauma Anaesthesia and Critical Care.
