01 Anticoagulation Bridging Protocol
Mechanical mitral valve = highest thromboembolic risk valve position. Never stop warfarin without bridging.

• Day −5: Stop warfarin
• INR <2.0 → Start enoxaparin 1 mg/kg SC BD (weight-adjusted)
• Day −1: Stop LMWH (24h before surgery)
• Day 0 morning: Confirm INR (was 1.1) → proceed
• Day 0 evening: Restart warfarin
• Continue LMWH bridge until INR therapeutic (confirmed Day 3)

Antibiotic endocarditis prophylaxis: amoxicillin 2 g IV + gentamicin 1.5 mg/kg — 30 min pre-induction. Mandatory for all prosthetic valve patients.

02 Airway Planning — Ramped Position + Video Laryngoscopy
Obesity: reduced FRC, increased upper airway soft tissue, more difficult mask ventilation and laryngoscopy.

Ramped position: 25° head-up, ear-to-sternal-notch alignment. Improves laryngoscopy grade AND extends apnoeic safe period (FRC optimised).

Pre-oxygenation: 5 min 100% FiO₂, tightly fitting mask.

Video laryngoscopy (C-MAC D-blade) as primary device → Grade I view obtained.

03 TIVA — Propofol TCI (Lean Body Weight) + Remifentanil
Opioid sparing is the cornerstone of OHS management — residual opioids profoundly depress the blunted ventilatory reserve.

Propofol TCI: use lean body weight — total body weight overestimates requirements → prolonged emergence. Effect-site target 2.5–3.0 mcg/mL maintenance.

Remifentanil: ester-hydrolysed in plasma and tissue → pharmacokinetics weight-independent. Ultra-short context-sensitive half-life → offset within minutes of stopping. Ideal for OHS.

04 Ventilation — PRVC + PEEP 8 cmH₂O
PRVC mode. TV 450 ml (6 ml/kg ideal body weight — lung-protective; never use total body weight for TV calculation).

PEEP 8–10 cmH₂O — maintains alveolar recruitment against the reduced FRC. Without PEEP, progressive atelectasis under GA in OHS is nearly inevitable → hypoxaemia.

ETT 8.0 mm, cuff pressure 25 cmH₂O. Arterial line for continuous BP. Subclavian CVC for vasopressor delivery.

05 Post-op — Immediate CPAP, No Opioids, Monitored Bed
Home CPAP applied in recovery room immediately post-extubation. Upper airway muscle tone recovers slowly after GA — risk of obstruction and hypoxaemia highest in first 30–60 min.

Analgesia: paracetamol 1g QDS + ketorolac 15 mg IV — opioids deliberately avoided.

Step-down monitored bed overnight. Continuous SpO₂. Cardiac medications (bisoprolol, furosemide) resumed Day 1.

📌 Key Teaching Points

❤️Mechanical mitral valve = highest thromboembolic risk position — always bridge when warfarin interrupted.
💉Bridging: warfarin Day −5 → LMWH when INR <2.0 → stop 24h pre-op → restart both post-op.
🦠Endocarditis prophylaxis mandatory for all prosthetic valve patients undergoing surgery.
😴OSA ≠ OHS: OHS = obesity + daytime PaCO₂ >45 — different ventilatory drive impairment.
💻TIVA with propofol TCI at lean body weight + remifentanil — preferred in OSA/OHS.
🛏️Ramped position (ear-to-sternal-notch) for pre-oxygenation and laryngoscopy.
💨Post-op: immediate CPAP, no opioids, monitored bed, overnight SpO₂.

🎤 Viva Questions

Why does a mechanical mitral valve require bridging when a bioprosthetic may not? ▶
Mechanical prostheses are inherently thrombogenic — synthetic surfaces promote thrombus formation at all times. The mitral position has higher risk than aortic (lower flow velocity, larger orifice). Bioprosthetic valves have lower intrinsic thrombogenicity. AHA/ACC 2014 and ESC 2021 guidelines both mandate bridging for high-risk mechanical valves when warfarin must be interrupted.
Why use lean body weight for propofol TCI in obesity, not total body weight? ▶
Propofol distributes predominantly into lean body mass and plasma. Using total body weight overestimates requirements because the excess weight is adipose tissue with comparatively low propofol uptake — this produces deeper anaesthesia and prolonged emergence. Lean body weight gives predictable emergence times. Remifentanil is pharmacokinetically weight-independent (ester hydrolysis) and doesn't require adjustment.
What is the physiological basis for PEEP 8–10 in OHS patients under GA? ▶
In OHS, FRC is already reduced by central adiposity pressing on the diaphragm. Under GA, FRC falls a further 15–20% from loss of respiratory muscle tone. This brings many OHS patients below their closing capacity → airway closure during tidal ventilation → progressive atelectasis and V/Q mismatch → hypoxaemia. PEEP 8–10 cmH₂O stents alveoli above closing capacity, maintains recruitment, and prevents intraoperative desaturation.
