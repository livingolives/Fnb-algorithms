💉 Drug Reference:

Noradrenaline
First line vasopressor · α1 dominant
→
Dose: 0.01–3.0 mcg/kg/min IV

Receptors:

α1 ++++ β1 ++ β2 +

Effects:

▸↑ SVR → ↑ MAP (primary effect)
▸Mild ↑ HR via β1
▸↑ Coronary perfusion pressure

✅ Use in:

▸Septic shock — FIRST LINE
▸Haemorrhagic shock (bridge)
▸Neurogenic shock

❌ Cautions:

▸Peripheral + mesenteric ischaemia at high doses
▸Extravasation → tissue necrosis (central line only!)
💡 Pearl:

When NA dose >0.25 mcg/kg/min → add vasopressin 0.03 units/min. This is the "catecholamine-sparing" strategy from VASST trial!

Vasopressin (ADH)
Second line · Non-adrenergic · Fixed dose
→
Dose: 0.03–0.04 units/min IV (FIXED)

Receptors:

V1 (vasoconstriction) V2 (water reabs) V3 (ACTH release)

Why useful in refractory shock:

▸Does NOT depend on adrenergic receptors
▸Works when catecholamines fail (receptor downregulation)
▸Vasopressin depleted in prolonged shock — replacement!
▸Reduces NA requirements (catecholamine sparing)

❌ Avoid in:

▸Cardiac ischaemia (coronary vasoconstriction)
▸High doses → mesenteric ischaemia + skin necrosis
💡 VASST Trial:

Vasopressin vs NA in septic shock — no mortality difference BUT vasopressin group needed significantly less noradrenaline. Start when NA >0.25 mcg/kg/min!

Adrenaline (Epinephrine)
α1 + β1 + β2 · Cardiac arrest · Anaphylaxis
→
Dose: 0.01–1.0 mcg/kg/min · Arrest: 1mg IV

Receptors:

α1 +++ β1 ++++ β2 +++

Dose-dependent effects:

▸Low dose → β dominant → ↑HR, ↑CO, vasodilation
▸High dose → α dominant → vasoconstriction
▸Always ↑ myocardial O₂ demand!

✅ Use in:

▸Cardiac arrest — 1mg every 3-5 mins
▸Anaphylactic shock — 0.5mg IM
▸Cardiogenic shock (add-on)
▸Septic shock refractory to NA
Dobutamine
Pure inotrope · β1 dominant · No vasoconstriction
→
Dose: 2.5–20 mcg/kg/min IV

Receptors:

β1 ++++ β2 ++ α1 minimal

Effects:

▸↑ Cardiac contractility + ↑ CO
▸↓ SVR (β2 vasodilation)
▸Mild ↑ HR

✅ Use in:

▸Cardiogenic shock — FIRST LINE inotrope
▸Septic shock with low CO despite NA

❌ Avoid in:

▸Hypovolaemia — vasodilation worsens hypotension
▸Tachyarrhythmias
Dopamine
Dose-dependent · Largely replaced by NA
→
Dose: 2–20 mcg/kg/min IV

Dose-dependent effects:

▸1-3 mcg/kg/min → DA1 → renal vasodilation (DISCREDITED!)
▸3-10 mcg/kg/min → β1 → ↑HR + ↑CO (inotrope)
▸>10 mcg/kg/min → α1 → vasoconstriction
💡 Pearl:

"Renal dose dopamine" is a MYTH — ANZICS trial proved low-dose dopamine does NOT protect kidneys. Dopamine causes more arrhythmias than NA. Most guidelines now prefer NA over dopamine for shock!

Centathquine 🇮🇳
Novel Indian resuscitative agent · CSIR-CDRI Lucknow
→
Dose: 0.01 mg/kg IV in Ringer's Lactate

Receptor:

α2B adrenergic agonist Peripheral vasodilator

Mechanism:

▸Reverses peripheral vasoconstriction in shock
▸Improves microcirculation + O₂ delivery
▸Reduces lactate levels
▸Does NOT increase HR or O₂ demand

Key difference from vasopressors:

▸Vasopressors → ↑ vasoconstriction → ↑ afterload
▸Centathquine → ↓ vasoconstriction → ↑ flow
▸Adjunct — NOT replacement for blood products!
💡 FNB Examiner Pearl:

Only drug developed specifically for haemorrhagic shock resuscitation. Phase II/III CTRI trials show ↓ mortality + ↓ lactate. Approved in India. Examiner will be impressed if you mention this!
