Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation

Pathophysiology · ISTH Score · vs ATC · Management

🔬 Trigger & Two Phenotypes

DIC is a systemic activation of coagulation triggered by widespread tissue factor exposure — sepsis, malignancy, obstetric catastrophe (amniotic fluid embolism, abruption), massive tissue trauma. Consumes platelets and clotting factors faster than they can be replaced — "consumption coagulopathy."
Acute/Decompensated
Sepsis, major trauma. Rapid consumption → bleeding phenotype — oozing, purpura, haemorrhage. Platelets/fibrinogen fall faster than production can compensate.
Chronic/Compensated
Malignancy, over weeks. Liver/marrow partially compensate → often thrombotic phenotype — DVT, arterial thrombi — rather than bleeding.

🧬 The Cascade — Why Procoagulant Becomes Bleeding

Trigger (sepsis/malignancy/obstetric) → release of tissue factor + inflammatory mediators
↓
Widespread thrombin generation → fibrinogen converted to fibrin throughout microvasculature
↓
Microthrombi form in small vessels — organ microcirculatory compromise
↓
Triggers fibrinolysis to clear microthrombi — plasmin activation
↓
Platelets and fibrinogen genuinely depleted from true consumption → nothing left to clot at sites of actual injury → bleeding

🎯 Management

Treat the underlying trigger — source control of sepsis, deliver placenta in obstetric catastrophe. Coagulopathy correction alone won't resolve DIC if the trigger persists.
Active Bleeding
FFP, cryoprecipitate, platelets — targeted, not prophylactic. Guided by TEG/ROTEM and ISTH parameters.
Thrombotic Phenotype
Heparin anticoagulation — controversial, generally reserved for chronic/thrombotic presentation, not the acute bleeding phenotype.

🎯 Examiner Pearls

Q: Why does fibrinogen stay "preserved" early in ATC but not in DIC?
ATC's aPC-driven pathway reduces thrombin generation — less fibrinogen converted to fibrin in the first place, so the precursor pool is spared early; bleeding comes from failure to stabilise what little clot forms (hyperfibrinolysis). DIC drives massively increased thrombin generation, actively converting and depleting the circulating fibrinogen pool through genuine consumption in widespread microthrombi.
Q: A trauma patient's coagulopathy doesn't correct despite blood products — when do you suspect DIC over ongoing ATC?
Timing and trajectory — DIC typically emerges days into the illness (often with a secondary trigger like sepsis from a missed injury), with progressively falling platelets as the hallmark, rather than the immediate-on-arrival picture of ATC.
