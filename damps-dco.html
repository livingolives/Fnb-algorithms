DAMPs & Damage Control Orthopaedics

Danger Signals · Second Hit Theory · Timing of Definitive Surgery

⚔️ DAMPs vs PAMPs

DAMPs
Damage-Associated Molecular Patterns
Endogenous — from your OWN damaged/necrotic cells
Trigger: tissue trauma, surgery, ischaemia
No infection required
PAMPs
Pathogen-Associated Molecular Patterns
Exogenous — from bacteria/pathogens (e.g. LPS/endotoxin)
Trigger: true infection, sepsis
Requires a pathogen
The clinical trap: both converge on the same receptors and downstream pathway — the innate immune system cannot distinguish "danger from bacteria" from "danger from tissue damage." A polytrauma patient without any infection can spike fevers, tachycardia, and meet SIRS criteria — looking septic without being septic.

🧪 Four Key DAMPs

Mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA)
Mitochondria are evolutionarily bacterial in origin — mtDNA is recognised by the same receptor (TLR9) as bacterial DNA. The direct mechanistic link between trauma and a sepsis-like response.
HMGB1
Nuclear protein released from necrotic cells. Binds RAGE and TLR4.
Heat Shock Proteins
Released from stressed/dying cells. Activate TLR2/TLR4.
ATP
Released from damaged cells. Signals via P2X7 purinergic receptors → inflammasome activation.
Broader category also includes: S100 proteins, hyaluronan fragments, uric acid.

🔬 The Shared Pathway

DAMPs released from damaged tissue
↓
Bind Toll-like Receptors — TLR4 and TLR9 (same as PAMPs)
↓
Activates NF-κB
↓
Pro-inflammatory cytokines — IL-6, IL-1β, TNF-α
↓
Same cascade as sepsis — SIRS without infection

🎯 The Second Hit Theory

First Hit — The Trauma Itself
Initial injury releases DAMPs → primes the innate immune system → systemic inflammatory response begins.
Vulnerable Window
Immune system already activated. Roughly the first 2–4 days post-injury.
Second Hit — Major Surgery Too Early
Fresh tissue trauma from definitive surgery releases more DAMPs on an already-primed system → risk of exaggerated response — ARDS, MODS.
Resolution — Day 5–10
Inflammatory surge settles. Definitive fixation now safer. Some centres individualise via IL-6, procalcitonin, or clinical trajectory rather than a fixed day.

📋 Worked Case

RTA · Maxillary + Left Tibial Fracture · Monday 8pm
Monday 8pm: Damage control ex-fix to tibia performed immediately — haemorrhage/contamination control, not the "second hit" surgery.

Tuesday morning: OMFS wants definitive maxillary fixation — deferred. Still within the peak inflammatory window from Monday's trauma.

Correct answer: defer definitive fixation ~5-6 days, until the inflammatory surge settles.
⚠ Don't Misread DCO
No surgery at all after Day 0 → Damage control surgery still proceeds immediately. Only major DEFINITIVE reconstructive surgery is deferred.

🎯 Examiner Pearls

Q: Why can trauma patients meet SIRS without infection?
DAMPs activate the same TLR4/TLR9 → NF-κB → cytokine pathway PAMPs use in sepsis. Tissue damage and infection converge on an identical inflammatory cascade.
Q: Why is mtDNA such an effective DAMP?
Mitochondria derive from bacteria (endosymbiotic theory) — mtDNA retains bacterial-like unmethylated CpG motifs recognised by TLR9, the same receptor detecting bacterial DNA.
Q: What determines definitive fixation timing beyond a fixed day count?
Inflammatory marker trends (IL-6, procalcitonin) and clinical trajectory increasingly individualise timing. 5–10 days is a practical guideline, not an absolute cutoff.
